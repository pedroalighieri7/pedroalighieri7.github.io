Bio

From the northeastern of Brazil, Pedro was born in Recife (PE) and spent most of his life in the city of Natal (RN). He graduated in Design from the University of Brasília (UnB), specializing in UX/UI. His first design works began in Paint. At the age of 9, he created logos for fictional companies, blogs on Blogspot, and Minecraft servers, as well as loved creating games on Scratch. In 2010, he started a small YouTube project with friends from Rio Grande do Norte. He participated in creating thumbnails for the channel, visual identity, and video editing. At its peak , “Noisjang” had more than 3 million views and 50 thousand subscribers. In high school , Pedro created several independent projects to help classmates with their studies. Among them, the project Liter, a forum aimed at improving essays.

In the artistic field, he participated in the BCE exhibition with Espaço POP, as well as in the White Page Gallery conceived by the MediaLab team from the University of Brasília. He developed the visual identity and helped set up the “From ICA to IdA” gallery in honor of the 50th anniversary of the UnB Art Institute.

In the professional field, he worked on improving the gov.br DS, the design system of the federal government, as well as creating systems and interfaces for institutions such as the Federal Revenue Service, IBAMA, among others. He is currently immersed in the world of UX/UI, HCI, WCAG, and AI. Pedro has experience in agile interface development within diverse teams. He believes that an accessible, ethical, and human approach is essential for creating a digital interface that truly works.
